A toolkit for high-throughput prediction of multicomponent phase diagrams

Combining first-principle based approaches with classical thermodynamic models and machine learning, I develop methods to rapidly predict composition-temperature phase diagrams for multinary materials.

Related tools & links:

  • Visualizing high dimensional spaces with SymPlex plots

Understanding order-disorder transitions in High-Entropy Alloys

Using DFT, Monte Carlo and phase-field simulations, I study the effect of short-range order and spinodal decomposition on mechanical properties in multicomponent alloys.

Related tools & links:

  • Monte Carlo based model to analyse short range order in multinary alloys
  • Design rules to tune microstructures in high entropy alloys via spinodal decomposition
  • Phase field simulations to analyze elastic energy contributions in spinodal decomposition

Investigating ferroelectricity in hafnium dioxide

Hafnia, already used in modern CMOS devices, can become the next generation of materials for memory devices and transistors. I study the methods by which ferroelectricity can be achieved in hafnia.
